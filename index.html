Brynn Sherman

I am a cognitive neursocientist broadly interested in learning and memory -- and in particular how they interact with one another. Specifically, I aim to understand how we use past memories to form general knowledge and make predictions about the future, and how that knowledge in turn influences how new memories are formed. I am also interested in how factors such as sleep and acute stress influence these learning and memory processes. I use a range of methods to approach these questions, including behavior, fMRI, and intracranial EEG.

I am currently a post doc in Anna Schapiro's lab at the University of Pennsylvania. I completed my graduate work with Nick Turk-Browne at Yale, where I also collaborated closely with Elizabeth Goldfarb. Before graduate school, I was an undergraduate and lab manager at New York University, where I worked with Lila Davachi and Sarah DuBrow on how we perceive and remember time.

Outside of research, I like to spend my time cooking, baking, or exploring Philadelphia. I also enjoy playing (and modifying) board games.
